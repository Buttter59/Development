The Basics

What is WPM?

Words Per Minute (WPM) is the standard measure of typing speed. In most typing tests a "word" is defined as five characters including spaces, making comparisons fair regardless of word length.

Formula: (Characters typed ÷ 5) ÷ Minutes elapsed. Example: 300 characters in 1 minute = 60 WPM.

Net WPM (what TyperTester shows) subtracts errors, rewarding accuracy alongside speed. A 100 WPM typist at 95% accuracy is more productive than a 120 WPM typist at 75% accuracy.

Consistency measures how stable your speed was throughout the test. High consistency (80%+) means steady rhythm — a sign of genuine skill.

How to Improve

10 proven ways to type faster

Tip 01
Learn Touch Typing
Use the Hand Guide above. Place fingers on home row (ASDF–JKL;) and never look at the keyboard. This single change unlocks dramatically higher speeds within weeks.
Tip 02
Accuracy Before Speed
Speed follows accuracy — never the reverse. Slow down until you can type at 98%+ accuracy, then gradually increase pace. Rushing creates bad habits that are hard to break.
Tip 03
Practice Daily
15–30 minutes of focused daily practice beats a 3-hour weekend session. Muscle memory builds through consistent repetition over time.
Tip 04
Use All Ten Fingers
Each finger has assigned keys — see the Hand Guide above. Left index covers F and G; right index covers J and H. Distributing the work enables real speed.
Tip 05
Type Real Text
Practicing with actual sentences and stories trains rhythm, punctuation habits, and real word patterns. TyperTester uses genuine passages for exactly this reason.
Tip 06
Read Ahead While Typing
Fast typists look ahead — reading the next word while their fingers handle the current one. This eliminates the micro-pause between words that slows most people down.
Tip 07
Fix Your Posture
Sit upright, keyboard at elbow height, monitor at eye level. Wrists should float — not rest — during typing. A good ergonomic setup removes physical friction from your practice.
Tip 08
Drill Your Weak Keys
Most typists have 3–5 keys they consistently mistype. Check your Error Heatmap on the Progress page to find which letters slow you down, then drill those specifically.
Tip 09
Increase Difficulty Gradually
Start Easy until you hit 50+ WPM, then move to Normal, then Hard. Level up when you can maintain 95%+ accuracy comfortably at the current level.
Tip 10
Track Your Progress
Seeing your WPM trend upward is one of the most motivating things in typing practice. Use the Progress page to watch your scores improve across days and weeks.
