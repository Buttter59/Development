Frequently Asked
Questions

Is TyperTester free? +
Yes — completely free, no hidden costs, no premium tiers. We don't even show ads.
Do I need an account? +
No. Your records are saved in your browser's local storage. No sign-up, no email, no password. Just open and play.
Will my scores be saved if I clear my browser? +
Records are stored in localStorage, so clearing your browser data will erase them. We recommend noting your best score elsewhere if you want a permanent record.
How is WPM calculated? +
We use the standard net WPM formula: (characters correctly typed ÷ 5) ÷ minutes elapsed. One "word" is always 5 characters including spaces, so the measurement is fair regardless of word length.
What's the difference between WPM and Raw WPM? +
Raw WPM counts all words attempted including errors. Net WPM (shown as your main score) subtracts incorrectly typed words to reflect your usable output — which is what matters in real-world typing.
Why do you use story passages instead of random words? +
Random words don't reflect real typing. Actual sentences contain natural word patterns, punctuation rhythm, and familiar phrases. Practicing with real text transfers more directly to everyday typing tasks.
What do the difficulty levels mean? +
Easy uses primary school vocabulary. Normal matches high school freshman level. Hard uses college-level academic language. Extreme features professional and technical prose — the kind found in philosophy, physics, and advanced technical writing.
Can I use TyperTester on mobile? +
TyperTester works on any device, but it's designed and optimized for keyboard use on laptops and desktops. Typing on a physical keyboard is the point — we recommend using a real keyboard for the best experience.
What does "consistency" mean in my results? +
Consistency measures how stable your WPM was throughout the test. A high consistency (80%+) means your speed was steady. Low consistency means you had significant bursts and slowdowns. Consistent typists are generally more reliable in real work.
How do I improve my WPM? +
The most effective approach: learn touch typing (home row, no looking at the keyboard), practice accuracy before speed, train daily in short focused sessions (15–30 min), and gradually increase difficulty. Check our full guide on the Learn page.
What is a good WPM? +
The global average is around 40 WPM. 60+ WPM is above average. 90+ WPM is considered professional. 120+ WPM is elite. Context matters too — a programmer at 70 WPM is entirely productive; a court reporter needs 225+ WPM on a stenograph.
