Our Story

Built by typists,
for typists.

TyperTester exists because the best typing tests we found were either full of ads, required accounts, used random word soup, or gave you no useful data after the test. We decided to fix that.

Mission

Why we built this.

Typing is the primary physical interface between human thought and digital output. Virtually every knowledge worker, student, writer, developer, and creator types for a large portion of their day. Yet most people never deliberately practice it — and never know how slow they actually are.

Most typing tests feel like chores. They're cluttered with ads, require sign-ups, present random disconnected words with no context, and give you a WPM number with nothing to do with it. You finish the test and walk away knowing one number but not understanding it, not knowing how to improve, and not feeling motivated to come back.

TyperTester is different. We use real passages — actual stories, science, history, and philosophy — so practice feels engaging rather than mechanical. We show you a speed graph so you can see your momentum over time. We compare your score to global benchmarks so you understand what your number actually means. And we give you a clear path forward through four difficulty levels that match real-world vocabulary complexity.

No account needed. No ads. No paywalled features. Free, forever.

Our Principles

What we believe in.

🚫
No ads. Ever.
Ads corrupt the experience. We don't sell your attention to advertisers. TyperTester is funded by goodwill, not impressions.
🔒
No account required.
Your records are saved in your browser locally. We don't collect your data. We don't know who you are. You just play.
📖
Real content, not random soup.
We use actual passages from real topics because context makes practice more interesting and more transferable to real-world typing.
📊
Data that teaches you something.
We don't just show you a number. We show you a chart, a comparison to global averages, your accuracy, consistency, and what tier you're in — because context is what makes data actionable.
